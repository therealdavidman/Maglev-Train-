## Required materials:

- A bench power supply
- A low-carbon dx8mm lx35mm steel rod
- 28 AWG wire
- 22 AWG pvc coated lead wires
- a multimeter
- kitchen scale
- a non metalic holder for the magnet
- A clicker
- soldering iron
- neodymium magnets
- a thermoucoupler
  

##Construction
- using the 28 AWG wire, begin tightly wrapping it around the steel rod leaving ~5 mm gap on each side of the rod.
- wrap tightly in a back and forth pattern making sure to not leave any gaps
- after the first 100 turns add a wire tap. then after every consecutive 50 turns add another tap.
- continue coiling until reaching 600 turns
- secure the thermocoupler to the middle of the electromagnet using kapton tape
- secure a neodymium magnet to the scale and the electromagnet to the non metalic holder


## Procedure:

### Preliminary characterization
  1. Photograph and measure the completed coil dimensions.
  2. Record:
      - core diameter and length;
      - winding length and ouside diameter;
      - turns availible at each tap;
      - ambient temperature
  3. Measure cold resistance from the start lead to every tap.
  4. Check resistance between the winding and steel core. it should appear elevtrically open.
  5. Determine permanent-magnet polarity and mart its orientation.
  6. Energize the electromagnet above the scale with no permanent magnet to measure interferance

  ### Tap screening at 4 mm

  For each selected tap:
  1. Allow the coil to return near ambient temperature
  2. Select the tap and insulate all unused taps.
  3. secure the permanent magnet to the scale and the coild to the rigid holder.
  4. set the gap (distance from core to the permanent magnet) to 4 mm using a non magnetic spacer
  5. with the coil off, record or tare the baseline scale reading.
  6. set the power-supply voltage low and set the desired current limit.
  7. Energize the coil and slowly raise the voltage until the target current is reached.
  8. record voltage, current, force, coil temperature, and gap.
  9. turn the power off as soon as the reading stabilizies
  10. repeat for currents:
      - 0.25A
      - 0.50A
      - 0.75A
      - 1.00A
  11. perform one trial per point initially.
  12. repeat any suspicious or inconsistent results.

  ### Force test

  choose the best tap from screening:

  1. Allow the coil to return near ambient temperature.
  2. Select the tap and insulate all others.
  3. Secure the magnet to the scale and the coil to the rigid holder.
  4. Set the gap to 2 mm using a nonmagnetic spacer.
  5. With the coil off, record or tare the basline scale reading.
  6. Set the voltage low and set the desired current limit.
  7. Energize the coil and slowly increase voltage until the target current is reached.
  8. Record voltage, current, force, coil temperature, and gap.
  9. turn the power off after the reading stabilized.
  10. repeat at 4, 6 , and 8 mm.
  11. Repeat each point at least 3 times.
  12. Repeat for the next current.
      test currents:
        0.10, 0.20, 0.30, 0.50, 0.75, 1.00 A

  ### Thermal test
  For each selected tap and current:

  1. Start with the coil near ambient temperature.
  2. Record cold resistance and ambient temperature
  3. Energize the coil at a fixed current.
  4. Record:
     - elapsed time;
     - temperature;
     - volage;
     - current
     - resistance calculated from R = V/I.
  5. Take readings every 30 seconds
  6. Stop at the temperature limit, maximum test time (5 minutes), or thermal equilibrium.
  7. Continue recording during cooldown

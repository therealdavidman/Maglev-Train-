
## DD-004

### Decsion

Choosing A low-carbon steel core instead of a pure soft iron core for testing.
### Reason

Pure soft iron of the required dimensions was difficult to source and signifigantly more expensive. Low-carbon steel was 
readily availible in the required size, allowing construction to proceed without delays.

### Tradeoffs

#### Pros:
- Much cheaper
- Availible in the required dimension.
- Shorter procurment time.

#### Cons:
- Slightly lower magnetic permability compared to soft iron.
- May reach magnetic saturation at a lower field strength
- Results may differ slightly from ideal soft iron core.

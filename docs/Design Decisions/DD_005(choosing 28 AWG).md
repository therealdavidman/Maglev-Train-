
## DD-005

### Decsion

Choosing 28 AWG magnetic wire
### Reason
- allowing 600 turns to fit on a 8mm x 35mm core
- producing sufficient magnetic field at moderate currents
- keeping coil resistance high enough that current can accuratley be controlled using a bench power supply
- allowing the coil to heat enough for meaningful thermal charactirization without immediatley exceeding
  safe operating temperatures

The gauge was selected as a compromise of number of turns, coil resistance, heating, and ease of winding.


### Tradeoffs

#### Pros:
- High number of turns increases magnetic field for a given current
- Easier to fit several hundred turns onto a compact core
- Higher resistance reduces the current required to achieve meaningful magnetic fields
- Thermal effects are easier to observe and model during testing
- Readily availible and inexpensive

#### Cons:
- Higher resistance causes greater (I^2)R heating
- Maximum continout current is lower than thicker wire
- Increased resistance results in a larger voltage drop
- More turns require additional winding time.

Although other wires might be suitable for the final levitation system, 28 AWG provides a good balance that allows
easy experimental characterization.

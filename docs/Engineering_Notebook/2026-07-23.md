# July 23, 2026

## Engineering Decisions Made Today
- Decided to transition from a repulsive electromagnetic suspension
  architecture to an attractive electromagnetic suspension architecture.
- Decided to modify the planned experiment to characterize attractive force
  instead of repulsive force.

## Objective
- Perform preliminary attraction-force measurements using the existing electromagnet.
- Determine whether an attractive architecture is capable of producing practical suspension forces.

## Accomplished
- Successfully performed preliminary attraction-force measurements.
- Measured approximately 150 gf of passive attraction at a 4 mm gap with no applied current.
- Measured approximately 300 gf of attraction at the same gap using only 0.4 A.
- Determined that the attractive configuration produced over an order of magnitude greater force than the repulsive configuration while requiring less current.


## Questions  
- How should the electromagnets be mechanically integrated beneath the track?
- How can the system prevent the electromagnets from contacting and sticking to the rail?
- Would passive magnetic buffers provide sufficient protection against contact?

## Next steps 
- Order a retort stand to improve experimental repeatability.
- Repeat the force experiment using the attractive architecture over the planned range of currents and air gaps.
- Begin redesigning the suspension system around the attractive configuration.
  
## End-Of-Day Reflection

### What did I accomplish?
Today marked a major breakthrough in the project. Preliminary testing showed that the attractive configuration generated approximately 
300 gf of force at a 4 mm gap using only 0.4 A, whereas the repulsive configuration previously produced only about 20–30 gf while requiring
significantly more current.
These results demonstrated that attraction is a much more practical approach for this project.

### What didn't work
Unfortunately, the experimental evidence strongly suggests that the original repulsive suspension architecture is not practical 
using the tested electromagnet configurations.

While redesigning the project was disappointing, making this decision now will likely save a significant amount of development time
and allow the project to move toward a more achievable solution.

### what did I learn?
I learned that experimental testing is just as valuable when a hypothesis fails as when it succeeds.

Rather than trying to force the original concept to work, the data showed that an attractive architecture provides substantially greater force 
with much lower electrical input. This demonstrated the importance of allowing experimental results to guide engineering decisions.

### What will i do next
Next I will redesign the experiment to fully characterize the attractive suspension system.
I will also document the reasoning behind the architectural change in my design decisions and begin investigating methods of preventing contact with the track,
such as passive magnetic buffers and mechanical safeguards.

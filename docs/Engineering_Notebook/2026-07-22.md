## End-Of-Day Reflection

### What did I accomplish?
Today I constructed and tested an air-core coil and a recessed hybrid air-steel core coil. 
Both were built successfully, allowing me to compare alternative magnetic geometries 
without relying only on the original full-core design.

### What didn't work
The air-core coil produced almost no usable force. Removing the steel core eliminated the unwanted induced attraction,
but it also removed most of the magnetic-field concentration provided by the ferromagnetic material.

The hybrid core produced the strongest repulsion of the alternatives tested, 
reaching approximately 30 gf at a 4 mm gap. However, this force remained far below what would be required for the intended levitation system.

### what did I learn?
I learned that the steel core creates both an advantage and a disadvantage. 
It greatly increases the electromagnet’s field, but the track magnet also magnetizes the core and biases the interaction toward attraction.

The hybrid design partly reduced this effect, but the improvement was not sufficient. Based on the preliminary results,
a purely electromagnetic repulsive architecture appears unlikely to meet the project requirements without a major redesign.

### What will i do next
Next, I will investigate two alternative directions: adding permanent magnets to provide passive repulsion or changing to an attractive electromagnetic suspension architecture. 
I will perform preliminary tests before deciding which architecture to pursue.

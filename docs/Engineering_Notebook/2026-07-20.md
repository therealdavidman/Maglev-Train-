## End-Of-Day Reflection

### What did I accomplish?
Today I wound and activated my first electromagnets. During the process, I learned how to prepare the core, 
insulate it, wind the coil, create taps, and make electrical connections. 
I also completed preliminary force testing. I got about 20 grams of repulsion at 1A which is very bad.

### What didn't work
The original repulsive design showed a major limitation. The permanent magnet induced magnetization in the steel core,
causing the unpowered core to be attracted to the magnet. 
As a result, the electromagnet first had to overcome this attractive bias before it could produce net repulsion.
This meant that the simplified model, which treated the permanent-magnet field and electromagnet field as directly additive,
did not accurately represent the real system.
